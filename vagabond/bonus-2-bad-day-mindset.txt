# THE BAD-DAY MINDSET GUIDE
## What to Do When Motivation Tanks

---

### THE TRUTH
You're not broken. You're not lazy. You're human.

Some days the motivation won't come. That's normal. The system isn't built on motivation — it's built on action.

This guide gives you the mental framework for those days.

---

### THE ONE REP RULE

**When you have zero energy, zero motivation, zero desire — do ONE rep.**

One pushup. One stretch. One breath. One rep.

That's it.

Why it works:
- Motivation follows action, not the other way around
- Once you do one rep, momentum takes over
- You're proving to your identity that you still show up

**Script to tell yourself:**
> "I don't have to do everything. I don't have to do a lot. I just have to do one thing."

---

### WHAT TO DO ON BAD DAYS

**Step 1: Acknowledge it**
Don't pretend you're fine. Say it out loud:
> "Today is hard. I'm still showing up."

**Step 2: The 5-Minute Rule**
Commit to 5 minutes only. Not a full workout. Just 5 minutes.
After 5 minutes, you can stop. Almost always, you won't stop.

**Step 3: One Physical Action**
Do exactly one physical thing:
- Stand up
- Put on your workout clothes
- Walk outside
- Do 5 squats

That's the whole system on bad days. One rep. 5 minutes. One action.

---

### THE VOICE IN YOUR HEAD

When you want to quit, the voice says:
- "Skip it today, start fresh tomorrow"
- "One day won't matter"
- "You're too tired"
- "It's not worth it"

**Here's the script to shut it down:**

> "This voice has spoken every time I've quit before. Every single time. And every time I listened, I stayed the same. I'm not listening today. One rep."

---

### RECOVERY DAYS ARE PART OF THE SYSTEM

If you genuinely need rest — not excuses, real recovery — then:

- Do 5 minutes of light stretching
- Walk 10 minutes
- Do the breathing exercise below

Rest is not weakness. But use it honestly.

---

### BREATHING EXERCISE (For Stress Days)

**Box Breathing:**
- Inhale 4 seconds
- Hold 4 seconds
- Exhale 4 seconds
- Hold 4 seconds
- Repeat 10 times

When your mind is scattered and your body is tired — breathe first. Everything else follows.
